Richter Earthquake Magnitude Scale

Understand the impact and precautions for each magnitude

1.0 - 2.9: Minor

Effects: Usually not felt, detected only by instruments.

Precautions for People:

  • Stay informed about local seismic activity.
  • Ensure emergency kits are prepared.
  • Learn about earthquake safety measures.

Actions for Authorities:

  • Monitor seismic data continuously.
  • Maintain public awareness campaigns.
  • Update earthquake preparedness guidelines.

3.0 - 3.9: Minor

Effects: Often felt indoors, like a light truck passing.

Precautions for People:

  • Stay calm and avoid running outdoors.
  • Secure loose objects on shelves.
  • Identify safe spots in your home.

Actions for Authorities:

  • Issue public notices if tremors are frequent.
  • Inspect critical infrastructure.
  • Ensure emergency communication systems are functional.

4.0 - 4.9: Light

Effects: Noticeable shaking, minor damage like rattling dishes.

Precautions for People:

  • Move away from windows and heavy furniture.
  • Secure heavy appliances to walls.
  • Practice "drop, cover, hold" drills.

Actions for Authorities:

  • Conduct structural inspections of buildings.
  • Prepare for potential aftershocks.
  • Activate local emergency response teams.

5.0 - 5.9: Moderate

Effects: Can cause damage to poorly built structures, felt widely.

Precautions for People:

  • Drop, cover, and hold under sturdy furniture.
  • Avoid elevators during and after shaking.
  • Check for gas leaks post-earthquake.

Actions for Authorities:

  • Assess and repair damaged utilities.
  • Deploy search and rescue teams if needed.
  • Establish temporary shelters for affected residents.

6.0 - 6.9: Strong

Effects: Destructive in populated areas, significant damage to buildings.

Precautions for People:

  • Evacuate unsafe buildings immediately.
  • Stay in open areas away from structures.
  • Prepare for aftershocks with emergency supplies.

Actions for Authorities:

  • Shut off gas and electricity in affected areas.
  • Coordinate rapid response and rescue operations.
  • Enforce safety zones around damaged buildings.

7.0 - 7.9: Major

Effects: Widespread severe damage, potential for collapse of structures.

Precautions for People:

  • Follow evacuation orders promptly.
  • Avoid coastal areas due to tsunami risk.
  • Stay tuned to emergency broadcasts.

Actions for Authorities:

  • Issue tsunami warnings if near coastlines.
  • Mobilize national emergency response teams.
  • Secure funding for immediate relief efforts.

8.0 - 8.9: Great

Effects: Catastrophic damage, near-total destruction in affected areas.

Precautions for People:

  • Seek immediate safety in open areas.
  • Prepare for long-term displacement.
  • Avoid damaged infrastructure like roads.

Actions for Authorities:

  • Request international aid for recovery.
  • Manage mass evacuations efficiently.
  • Restore critical services like water and power.

9.0+: Great

Effects: Devastating over large areas, rare and catastrophic.

Precautions for People:

  • Survive initial shaking in safe zones.
  • Prepare for extended loss of services.
  • Follow government relocation plans.

Actions for Authorities:

  • Coordinate global relief and reconstruction.
  • Rebuild critical infrastructure.
  • Provide long-term housing solutions.
